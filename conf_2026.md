* Diagnostic accuracy, fairness and clinical implementation of AI for breast cancer screening: results of multicenter retrospective and prospective technical feasibility studies `Nature Cancer` `Google`
	> Motivation: Evaluate Google AI model as a reader in double-reading (EU/UK) settings.
	
	> Findings: AI achieved superior sens and non-inferior specs compared to first reader. Cancer detection rate increased. No systematic demographic disparities were observed. The AI model reduces reading time while increasing detection.

* Identification of Aspergillus at section and species levels by artificial intelligence-based microscopic morphology image recognition `Clinical Microbiology` `CN` `Academia`
	> The paper proposes fungalNet a deep network model that identifies fungus at section (coarse) and specie (fine)-grained levels with high accuracy. To train fungalNet, the authors curated a dataset of 11,689 high-resolution 1920×1200 microscopy images. The dataset spans 8 sections and 12 species. The paper emphasizes the importance of high-quality images for training and evaluation as well as the importance for high (100x) magnificantion level.
	
* Molmo2 Open Weights and Data for Vision-Language Models with Video Understanding and Grounding `AI2` `UW` `US`
	> Motivation: Develop fully open-source (model, training data) VLM models -- with video processing capabilities  -- that either matches or beats proprietary.
	
	> Method: The paper uses standard VLM architecture: vision encoder, LLM, and VL connector. The key contributions are: (1) nine newly curated dataset for captioning, pointing, QA with long videos, (2) very efficient training tricks to handle very long context (videos). The efficient training tricks includes: sequence packing, token weighting, Ulysses attention, and message trees. Sequence packing and token weighting are easy -- to understand -- ideas that have been explored in previous literature. Ulysses attention allows context parallelism across multiple GPUs; so long video can be processed -- by vision encoder -- on multiple GPUs. Message trees is a method to batch/collate single image with multiple annotations (QA) efficiently. The key idea is to encode the image once through the vision encoder and process with all (multiple) text annotations (caption/QA) simultanously while preventing cross-branch (text annotation) attention. The paper causally mention that their model uses bi-directional attention across image tokens.


## arXiv

* Mirage: The Illusion of Visual Understanding `Stanford` `US` `Academia`

	> The paper coins the term mirage reasoning which is similar to hallucination. In hallucination, the model fills in ungrounded details given an image. However, mirage reasoning happens when a model generates reasoning/thinking output when no image is provided. In the mirage mode, the model's output (1) indicate full confidence, (2) achieve high score on many benchmarks. The paper attributed the high performance score to (1) data leakage, (2) vision-independent QA benchmarks and (3) other latent cues (e.g., hidden structures in benchmarks). In medical image benchmarks, mirage-based outputs tends to be alarming, biased towards heavily-pathology, time-senstive, and resource-intensive diagnosis. The most-alarming aspect of mirage responses is their silent failing mode. E.g., an erroneous upload or forgetting an input image causes a silent failure generating a coherent answer with a careful reasoning trace.
	
	> The paper suggest multiple mirage-mitigation approaches: (1) B-Clean: Revise benchmarks and eliminate any questions that can be answered without access to images, (2) using other agents (models) to compare the model's output across different scenarios (counterfactuals, rephrased queries, and the respective mirage-mode), (3)  modality-ablation testing ,(3) private or dynamically-updated benchmarks
